# Figure Legends

**Figure 1 | SafeMemBench-Action evaluates policy carriage at the action boundary.** **a,** A long-horizon agent receives policy-bearing information from user instructions, organizational memory, policy documents, history and retrieved memory before proposing a candidate tool action. Policy carriage can be preserved, absent, weakened, misbound or over-included. **b,** Each episode separates canonical policies, corruption artifacts, a noisy policy pool, carried policy content and offline-only evaluation annotations. **c,** The benchmark contains 300 bilingual episodes across six tool domains, with 180 risky and 120 safe actions.

**Figure 2 | Policy-carriage states produce distinct action errors.** **a,** Failure--error matrix for carried-policy context on the completed 300-episode DeepSeek run. Absent, weakened and misbound states predominantly yield unsafe allows or under-blocked confirmations, whereas over-included policies refuse all safe cases. **b,** Aggregate accuracy, executed-violation rate and false-refusal rate for no policy, carried policy, full clean replay, clean MSR, full noisy replay and noisy MSR. **c,** The same comparison for Qwen. Values are exact episode proportions from temperature-zero runs.

**Figure 3 | Minimal retrieval reduces clean-context cost but does not solve noisy-pool false refusal.** **a,** Policy-token cost and average retrieved-policy count for full replay and MSR under clean and noisy policy sources. **b,** Accuracy and false-refusal rate for the two completed decision models. Clean MSR matches clean replay while using 23.15 rather than 958.87 policy tokens per episode; under noise, both methods retain approximately one-fifth false refusal. **c,** Policy-state breakdown of noisy-retrieval errors.

**Supplementary Figure 1 | Real context compression can reproduce policy-carriage failures.** **a,** Human-labeled policy-state distribution for five compression methods applied to eight long-context seeds per method. **b,** Downstream decision accuracy, executed-violation rate and false-refusal rate. Truncation removed the operative policy in all eight seeds and produced three unsafe executions. This small sanity check assesses methodological plausibility and is not used to estimate population prevalence.
